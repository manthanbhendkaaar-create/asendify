How We Escalated Four Channels

Imperial · OverSimplified · Older Brother · MegaloMedia — one retention-engineering system, four different niches, consistent escalation.

The System

The Retention Engineering Framework

The same four-stage system underpins every escalation on this page.

01

Retention Diagnostic

  • Frame-by-frame audit of the audience retention curve to find exact drop-off points in existing uploads.
02

Editing System Overhaul

  • Rebuild pacing, cut density, and visual language around what the data says keeps viewers watching.
03

Hook & Structure Redesign

  • Re-engineer the first 15 seconds and mid-video structure, then test and iterate against real watch-time.
04

Consistency & Compounding

  • Lock in publishing cadence plus thumbnail/title testing so the algorithm compounds gains over time.
The Pipeline

From Idea to Published Video

The same start-to-finish pipeline runs behind every channel we work with. Typical turnaround: under 48 hours from ideation to a publish-ready video.

01–04

Pre-Production (Day 0–1)

  • Ideation — topics and angles chosen from niche trends and retention data before anything is written.
  • Script Writing — full script drafted from the chosen idea, structured for hooks and pacing.
  • Voiceover Recording — recorded once the script is locked and approved internally.
  • Edit & Animation — footage, B-roll, and motion graphics assembled around the retention framework.
05–08

Production, Review & Delivery (Day 1–2)

  • Thumbnail & Title — multiple options designed and tested alongside the edit.
  • Internal QC — draft checked against brand tone, audio levels, captions, and hook strength.
  • Client Review — draft plus thumbnail/title options sent for feedback; revisions turned same-day.
  • Delivery & Publish — final video and approved thumbnail/title delivered, ready to publish.
Results

Four Channels, Four Escalations

Finance / Markets Channel

Imperial

2.5x growth in 8 months
200K 500K
  1. Diagnosed the drop-off — Retention curves showed viewers exiting during slow market-recap intros.
  2. Rebuilt the cut — Tighter, cinematic pacing matched to Imperial's dark, high-stakes brand tone.
  3. Re-cut the hook — Cold open on the stakes/thesis in the first 10s instead of a greeting.
  4. Locked cadence — Consistent upload schedule plus thumbnail A/B testing compounded the gains.
History / Storytelling Channel

OverSimplified

900x growth in 14 months
10K 9M
  1. Diagnosed the drop-off — Long historical exposition was losing viewers before the narrative payoff.
  2. Rebuilt the cut — Restructured episodes into serialized story-beats edited for narrative momentum.
  3. Re-cut the hook — Every video opens mid-conflict, deferring context until viewers are already invested.
  4. Locked cadence — Predictable release rhythm and consistent thumbnail language built a returning audience.
Mentorship / Advice Channel

Older Brother

2.36x growth in 5 months
50K 118K
  1. Diagnosed the drop-off — Talking-head segments lost viewers past the 3-minute mark with no visual variety.
  2. Rebuilt the cut — Layered in B-roll, on-screen text, and pattern interrupts to sustain long-form watch time.
  3. Re-cut the hook — Opened with the advice's payoff/result, then unpacked the reasoning behind it.
  4. Locked cadence — Weekly release rhythm plus title testing rebuilt algorithmic trust over time.
Finance / Education Channel

MegaloMedia

4.86x growth in 7 months
50K 243K
  1. Diagnosed the drop-off — Viewers dropped off mid-video where dense concepts weren't visually reinforced.
  2. Rebuilt the cut — Structured chapters and on-screen breakdowns turned dense topics into a clear flow.
  3. Re-cut the hook — Led with the concrete outcome/number before the explanation.
  4. Locked cadence — Fixed publishing schedule and thumbnail/title testing compounded reach.
